# DECISION: Final Scope and Assessment Alignment

## Decision

Earlier journal entries used the working title "Food App" and explored a broader two-sided marketplace concept. The final individual assignment scope is SurplusSense as a merchant-side F&B surplus decision cockpit.

## Rationale

This scope decision was made because MGMT655 assesses a working AI/ML decision-support product, not the breadth of a full marketplace venture.

The merchant cockpit better demonstrates the ML decision-making competencies the course evaluates:

- supervised ML surplus prediction
- leakage-aware temporal validation
- rule-based recommendation logic
- food-safety governance
- explainable decision output
- commercial pilot logic and unit economics

## Human Judgment Applied

COC and AI assistance generated several possible directions including marketplace features and broader platform concepts. The decision to narrow the product to the merchant decision layer was a deliberate human judgment — a focused decision-support product is more credible, testable, and aligned with the individual assignment rubric than a diluted marketplace concept.

## What Is Not Claimed

The final submission does not claim that SurplusSense currently has:

- real merchant adoption
- production deployment
- live POS integration
- consumer marketplace liquidity
- payment or fulfilment infrastructure

Those are Phase 2 extensions. The submission honestly positions the product as pilot-ready.

## Final Assessment Position

The final submission should be evaluated as a pilot-ready ML decision-support product. It is not yet production-validated, but it is mature enough for a merchant, investor, or company sponsor to evaluate — and clearly demonstrates the ML decision-making competencies the MGMT655 individual assignment assesses.
