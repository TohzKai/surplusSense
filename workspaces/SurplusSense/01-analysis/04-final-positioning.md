# Final Positioning: From Marketplace to Merchant Decision Cockpit

## Background

Earlier analysis explored SurplusSense as a two-sided food-surplus marketplace. That concept had commercial potential but also introduced unnecessary scope: consumer acquisition, payments, delivery, inventory integration, and marketplace liquidity.

## Final Positioning Decision

For the individual assignment, the product was narrowed to a merchant-side decision-support cockpit.

The final product answers a sharper operational question:
"What should the merchant do with predicted surplus inventory before it loses value?"

## Why the Merchant Cockpit Is Stronger for MGMT655

The merchant cockpit better satisfies the MGMT655 individual assignment because it foregrounds:

- supervised ML prediction (XGBoost surplus regression)
- decision-support workflow (prediction → action)
- explainable recommendation logic (rule-based, not black-box)
- food-safety governance (safety overrides commercial optimisation)
- business-case reasoning (unit economics, recovery value)
- pilot-ready validation (usage metrics, recovery targets, willingness-to-pay)

A full marketplace would be broader but less focused on machine-learning decision support and harder to demonstrate as a working product within the course timeline.

## What Was Rejected

The final MVP does not include a consumer marketplace because it would require:

- consumer acquisition at scale
- payment flows and settlement
- order fulfilment and logistics
- inventory integration across merchant systems
- marketplace liquidity management

These are valid venture requirements but not the strongest evidence for this assignment's AI/ML decision-making objectives.

## Final Product Boundary

**Final MVP:**
Input item context → Predict surplus → Apply recommendation rules → Apply food-safety gate → Estimate recovery value → Explain decision to merchant

**Phase 2:**
Marketplace listing, consumer app, POS integration, payments, delivery, and ESG reporting modules.

## Assessment Implication

This scope decision strengthens the assignment because it shows deliberate judgment. The final product scope was a deliberate narrowing to the highest-value decision-support layer that could be demonstrated as a working AI/ML product — not an accidental inconsistency from the early marketplace exploration.
